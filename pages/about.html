About Michael

I earned my PhD in Rhetoric and Composition in August of 2021 from Florida State University. I work as a Visiting Teaching Faculty member at FSU where I teach college composition and courses in the Editing, Writing, and Media concentration in the English Major. in scholarship from the 1990s. My dissertation, Tracing Techne: Distributed Histories of Invention, Creativity, and Text-Technologies in Rhetoric and Composition Scholarship from the 1990s utilized the digital humanities and data feminism to study the histories and historograpies techne through the concept's presences and impressions within a corpus of field journals. My research interests are at the intersections of the digital humanities, data feminism, techne, and historiography, with a particular focus on the roles of history have on shaping conceptions about techne and how those histories shape writing curriculums and pedagogies. I also have interests in rhetoric and code, design, creative nonfiction, play, convergences between creative writing and composition pedagogies, and the undergraduate major in writing and rhetoric.

I am originally from Omaha Nebraska, where I completed my BA in English with a Creative Writing Focus at Creighton University in 2010. I received an MA in English from the University of Nebraska-Omaha in 2016. When I'm not teaching, researching, or writing, I enjoy running, cooking, and watching Star Trek with Jessi and our two golden retrievers, Mardi and Janeway.

I have crafted this ePortfolio as a showcase of my projects and interests, and as a place to assemble and practice the many different digital and networked tools and technologies that I use in my teaching, writing, and research. You can follow the menu at the top right to get to different sections of the ePortfolio.
